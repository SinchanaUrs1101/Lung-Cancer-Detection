Lung Cancer

About

Lung cancer is a cancer that develops in the lungs, usually in the cells that line the air passages. It's the leading cause of cancer death in both men and women.
Lung cancer happens when cells in the lung change (or mutate). Most often, this is because of exposure to dangerous chemicals that we breathe. But lung cancer can also happen in people with no known exposure to toxic substances. Unlike normal cells, cancer cells grow uncontrollably and cluster together to form a tumor, destroying healthy lung tissue around them. Symptoms usually do not appear until cancer cells spread to other parts of the body and prevent other organs from functioning properly. At this point, it is harder to treat lung cancer.

Early Detection Is Important

Though lung cancer is common, many people don't know much about it or are afraid to learn. Lung cancer can go undiagnosed for a long time because symptoms usually do not appear until the cancer has spread. If you are over the age of 50 and currently smoke,or have quit within the last 15 years, you may be at high risk and be eligible for lung cancer screening.

  • Causes:Smoking is the leading cause of lung cancer, responsible for about 85% of all cases. Other risk factors include exposure to dangerous chemicals, radon gas, air pollution, and previous chest problems or lung diseases.
  • Symptoms:Symptoms usually don't appear until the cancer has spread to other parts of the body. Symptoms include chest pain, unexplained weight loss, and loss of appetite.
  • Types:The two main types of lung cancer are non-small cell lung cancer and small cell lung cancer.
  • Diagnosis:A doctor will ask about your health history, perform a physical exam, and may order tests like blood tests, imaging scans, and lung function tests.
  • Treatment:Lung cancer is often diagnosed at advanced stages when treatment options are limited.
  • Prevention:Reducing exposure to environmental risk factors and tobacco control measures can help reduce the incidence of lung cancer.
